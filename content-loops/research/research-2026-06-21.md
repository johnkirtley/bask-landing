## NEW #A — Does Sunlight Increase Testosterone? (What the Research Actually Shows)
**Priority: HIGH — strongest new find this cycle. Best new install-driver since it sits squarely in the biohacker audience.**

- **Topic / intent:** Informational with strong male-health/biohacker bias. Searchers (r/biohackers, r/hubermanlab, r/supplements, r/testosterone) want a yes/no plus a mechanism and a protocol. High, durable, year-round volume that spikes in summer.
- **Primary keyword:** does sunlight increase testosterone
- **Secondary / long-tail:** sun exposure and testosterone · sunlight testosterone study · does the sun boost testosterone · UV light testosterone
- **Why we can rank (verified SERP read):** First page is **TRT clinics, supplement sellers, and hormone blogs** (rethinktestosterone, honehealth, hormone-zone, sogevity, goldmanlaboratories) — commercially motivated, thin on primary citations, no NIH/Mayo/Healthline wall. There is **one strong primary source nearly all of them paraphrase loosely** (the 2021 *Cell Reports* skin-brain-gonad paper). A properly-sourced, honest piece that reads the actual study and separates hype from evidence can outrank these and is highly LLM-citable.
- **SEO/GEO build:**
  - Direct-answer snippet: *Yes, but modestly and indirectly.* Sun exposure is **associated** with higher testosterone via two routes — a UVB→skin→brain→gonad signalling pathway and the vitamin D pathway — but the human evidence is early/observational, and **burning yourself does not raise it faster.**
  - Table: mechanism → what the evidence shows → how strong it is (UVB skin-brain-gonad axis | vitamin D → free-T | seasonal T variation).
  - FAQ schema: "Does the sun boost testosterone?", "How much sun for testosterone?", "Does vitamin D raise testosterone?", "Is morning or midday sun better for testosterone?"
- **Honest framing / Bask fit (YMYL — be careful):** Do NOT promise a T boost. The honest spine: the headline studies are small/observational, the human data is suggestive not proven, and the *behaviour* that the evidence supports is **regular, moderate sun (the ~20–30 min, few-times-a-week pattern), not maximal exposure.** That is exactly Bask's dose model — same "right amount, not maximum" message as the vitamin D core, aimed at a high-intent, app-friendly audience. Bask times moderate daily exposure to skin type + live UV.
- **Must-cite / verify:**
  - Andersen/Slominski-adjacent **2021 *Cell Reports*, "Skin exposure to UVB light induces a skin-brain-gonad axis and sexual behavior"** (Tel Aviv / Ben-Gurion). **VERIFY:** human cohort was small and partly UVB-phototherapy patients; state the limitation plainly. Confirm whether the human T correlation is seasonal/observational vs. interventional.
  - A vitamin-D-and-testosterone source for the indirect pathway (cite a specific human trial; **flag that several vitamin-D-and-T RCTs are null** — do not overstate).
  - NIH ODS for vitamin D baseline.
- **Original angle:** Read the *actual* study and grade the evidence honestly ("associated, modest, early") instead of the clinic-blog "sun = more T" overclaim — that honesty is the rankable gap and the natural setup for Bask's moderate-dose message.

## NEW #B — Does a Base Tan Protect You From Sunburn? (No — Here's the SPF Math)
**Priority: MEDIUM-HIGH. Builds the tanning cluster with 06-20 #2 and #5; pure summer myth-bust; textbook Operating-Principles post.**

- **Topic / intent:** Myth / safety, high summer volume. People plan to "build a base tan before vacation." ~1 in 4 adults believe it protects them — a large, correctable audience.
- **Primary keyword:** does a base tan protect from sunburn
- **Secondary / long-tail:** base tan myth · does a base tan prevent sunburn · what spf is a base tan · is a base tan safe before vacation
- **Why we can rank:** SERP is a mix of strong health sites (Harvard Health, MD Anderson, Scientific American) **and** product/affiliate blogs — tougher than #A, but the query is a clean yes/no with a quantified answer (the SPF number), which is ideal featured-snippet/AI-Overview shape and an easy LLM citation. Our edge is the **SPF-3 math stated as a single liftable line** plus internal links from the tanning cluster (#2, #5) and the sunscreen post (does-sunscreen-block-vitamin-d, already shipped).
- **SEO/GEO build:**
  - Direct-answer snippet: **No.** A base tan provides roughly **SPF 3 or less** (U.S. Surgeon General) — versus SPF 15+ recommended — and it *is itself* UV damage. You still burn; you just added cumulative damage first.
  - Table: protection source → approximate SPF (base tan ~3 | salon tan <1 | SPF 30 sunscreen 30) → real protection?
  - FAQ schema: "What SPF is a base tan?", "Does a base tan prevent burning?", "Is a base tan safe before vacation?", "Does a spray tan protect from sun?"
- **Honest framing / Bask fit:** Straight, evidence-led correction — no fear-mongering, just the math. The Bask bridge is light: a base tan is the skin's damage response and buys almost no protection, so the real lever is **timing and dose**, not pre-tanning. Bask reads live UV + skin type so you get the short vitamin D window without the burn the "base tan" plan invites. (Conversion is softer than #A; value here is cluster authority + an honest, citable answer.)
- **Must-cite / verify:** U.S. Surgeon General / Skin Cancer Foundation for the **SPF ≈ 3** figure (**verify the exact SPF claim and attribution** — blogs cite 2–4 loosely); MD Anderson / Harvard Health / Scientific American for the myth-bust; a "UV damage is cumulative" source. **Verify the "1 in 4 adults believe it" stat to a named survey before using it.**
- **Original angle:** Lead with the concrete SPF-3 number as the whole answer (most posts bury it in prose) and tie it to the same "timing beats more sun" thesis as the rest of the cluster.
