The query rewards a single clean minutes-by-Fitzpatrick table — exactly the liftable block LLMs quote. Internal-link equity from the shipped cluster is our edge.
- **SEO/GEO build:**
  - Snippet answer up top with the real ranges (fair I–II ~10–15 min, medium III–IV ~15–25 min, dark V–VI ~40–60+ min at UV ≥3).
  - Centerpiece **minutes-by-skin-type × UV index (3/5/7) table** — the citable asset.
  - FAQ schema: "How long for dark skin to get vitamin D?", "Does tanned skin make less vitamin D?", "Why do I burn before I tan?", "Can darker skin get enough vitamin D from the sun?"
- **Honest framing / Bask fit:** Lead with the **dark-skin vitamin D gap** (higher deficiency prevalence — a real equity angle most posts skip) and kill the "stay out until slightly pink" target — pinkness is the start of a burn, not a vitamin D milestone, and burning adds _zero_ extra D. That honest correction is the setup: Bask personalizes the timer to your Fitzpatrick type and live UV so you hit the dose without overshooting into burn territory.
- **Must-cite / verify:** NIH ODS (skin-tone effect on synthesis); a melanin/MED source (Holick); deficiency-prevalence-by-skin-tone stat (cite a specific study — verify the number before publishing).

## #2 — What UV Index Is Best for Tanning (and What It Actually Does for Vitamin D)

**Priority: HIGH. Strongest discovery find this cycle.** _(New — not in the original pillar plan.)_

- **Topic / intent:** Mixed informational/transactional from the **tanning audience** (r/tanning is a named source community). People want a "good UV to tan" number; they also half-believe tanning = vitamin D. Big, live summer volume.
- **Primary keyword:** best UV index for tanning
- **Secondary / long-tail:** what UV index is good for tanning · can you tan and get vitamin D at the same time · does a tan mean you got vitamin D · is UV index 5 good for tanning
- **Why we can rank (verified SERP read):** The first page is **clinic blogs and tanning-product/affiliate sites** (Aventus, SwimZip, IAPAM, Talking Tan, Purish) — no NIH/Mayo/Healthline wall. An accurate, properly-sourced piece that treats the health side honestly can outrank these and is highly LLM-citable because the existing pages are thin on real citations.
- **SEO/GEO build:**
  - Snippet answer: WHO frames UV **3–5** as the lower-risk tanning range, but state plainly there's no "safe" tan, and that **the UV that tans you (UVA-heavy) is not the same as the UVB that makes vitamin D** — so a tan is not proof you made any D.
  - Table: UV index band → tanning speed → burn risk → vitamin D production. One table does triple duty.
  - FAQ schema: "Is UV index 5 good for tanning?", "Can you tan and get vitamin D at the same time?", "Does a tan mean you got vitamin D?", "What UV index is too high?"
- **Honest framing / Bask fit (YMYL — be careful):** Don't endorse tanning. The honest spine: a tan is the skin's damage response; vitamin D and a cosmetic tan are _different photobiology_; burning gets you neither faster D nor a better tan, just risk. That's the bridge — Bask reads live UV and your skin type to time the short window that actually makes vitamin D, and warns before you cross into burn time. Captures the large tanning-curious audience and redirects it to the health-first product.
- **Must-cite / verify:** WHO INTERSUN / UV Index guidance (the 3–5 framing); Skin Cancer Foundation ("a tan is a sign of skin damage"); a UVA-vs-UVB source for the "tan ≠ vitamin D" mechanism. **Verify the WHO 3–5 attribution** — several blogs cite it loosely.
- **Original angle:** Reframes a high-intent tanning query through honest photobiology + Bask's live-UV mechanic — a genuine first-page gap.

## #3 — How Much Vitamin D Do You Actually Get in 15 Minutes of Sun? (P1.9)

**Priority: HIGH.**

- **Topic / intent:** Calculation. "Is a quick 15 in the sun enough?" — perfect featured-snippet / AI-Overview shape, peak summer relevance.
- **Primary keyword:** how much vitamin D in 15 minutes of sun
- **Secondary / long-tail:** vitamin D 10 minutes sun · IU vitamin D per minute sun · is 15 minutes of sun enough vitamin D · how many IU is 20 minutes of sun
- **Why we can rank:** Direct-number queries reward a tight quantified answer + table; we can deliver real IU ranges with primary sourcing where competitors hand-wave. Links up to cornerstone and over to #1 (skin type) and shipped UV-index post.
- **SEO/GEO build:**
  - Snippet answer with a real range: fair skin, most of body, midday summer (UV 6+) ≈ several thousand IU in ~15 min — often more than a typical supplement dose; darker skin / lower UV / less skin exposed drops it to a few hundred IU or less.
  - **IU-in-15-minutes by skin type × UV table**, plus a "15 min of sun vs a 1,000–2,000 IU pill" comparison row.
  - FAQ schema: "Is 15 minutes of sun enough vitamin D?", "How many IU is 20 minutes of sun?", "Does 10 minutes count?", "Can a short walk give me vitamin D?"
- **Honest framing / Bask fit:** The honest caveat is that "15 minutes" is meaningless without UV, skin type, and exposed-skin area — the same trap the cornerstone calls out. Use it to justify why an app that knows all three beats a fixed number. Bask estimates IU per actual session.
- **Must-cite / verify:** Holick's canonical full-body 1-MED ≈ 10,000–25,000 IU figure (web search surfaced this repeatedly — **verify exposed-skin assumptions**, since full-body ≠ arms/legs and most readers won't expose full body). NIH ODS. Be precise that the big IU numbers assume large skin area.
- **Original angle:** Honest "the big IU numbers assume full-body exposure" correction — most posts quote 10,000–25,000 IU without noting it's near-naked midday sun.

## #4 — Can You Get Too Much Vitamin D From the Sun? (P1.12)

**Priority: MEDIUM-HIGH. Strong seasonal safety/reassurance fit.**

- **Topic / intent:** Safety / myth-bust. Summer sunbathers worry about overdoing it. The honest answer is reassuring on D but pointed on burns — pure Operating-Principles territory.
- **Primary keyword:** can you get too much vitamin D from the sun
- **Secondary / long-tail:** vitamin D toxicity from sun · sun vitamin D overdose · how much sun is too much · can sunbathing cause vitamin D toxicity
- **Why we can rank:** Clean yes/no with a mechanism = ideal snippet/LLM answer. Moderate competition; our cluster links give an edge.
- **SEO/GEO build:**
  - Snippet answer: **No — you can't overdose on vitamin D from sunlight.** Skin self-limits cutaneous D and breaks down the excess; toxicity only comes from high-dose supplements. The real risk from too much sun is burning and skin damage, not too much D.
  - "Where toxicity actually comes from" (supplements) vs "the real danger of overexposure" (burns, photoaging, skin-cancer risk) contrast.
  - FAQ schema: "Can sunbathing cause vitamin D toxicity?", "How much sun is too much?", "What are signs of too much vitamin D?"
- **Honest framing / Bask fit:** This is the thesis post — overexposure is exactly the problem Bask solves. You physically _can't_ get more D by staying out longer once you're capped, so extra time is pure downside. Bask shows the point of diminishing returns and flags burn risk.
- **Must-cite / verify:** NIH ODS (cutaneous synthesis self-limits; toxicity is supplement-driven, hypercalcemia thresholds); Skin Cancer Foundation / WHO for overexposure risks.
- **Original angle:** Frames the self-limiting cap as a _behavioral_ argument ("longer = zero extra D, all extra risk"), tied to a live readout.

## #5 — Do Tanning Beds Give You Vitamin D? (P1.14)

**Priority: MEDIUM. Captures the tanning-adjacent / r/tanning audience; pairs with #2.**

- **Topic / intent:** Myth / safety. Recurrent belief that sunbeds are a vitamin D shortcut.
- **Primary keyword:** do tanning beds give vitamin D
- **Secondary / long-tail:** tanning bed vitamin D · sunbed vitamin D · is a tan a sign of vitamin D · tanning vitamin D
- **Why we can rank:** Clear factual myth-bust; SERP is tanning-salon marketing (which has an incentive to muddy this) + a few health sites — an honest, sourced answer stands out and earns LLM citations.
- **SEO/GEO build:**
  - Snippet answer: **Mostly no** — commercial tanning beds emit mainly UVA (tans/ages skin), not the UVB that makes vitamin D, and health authorities don't recommend them for D because they raise skin-cancer risk without reliably raising levels.
  - FAQ schema: "Do sunbeds make vitamin D?", "Is a tan a sign of vitamin D?", "Are tanning beds ever recommended?", "What's safer than a tanning bed?"
- **Honest framing / Bask fit:** Straight myth correction; redirect to safe ways to raise low D (timed sun, food, supplements). Bask = the safe-sun-timing option.
- **Must-cite / verify:** WHO / IARC (tanning beds Group 1 carcinogen); Skin Cancer Foundation; NIH ODS (UVA vs UVB). **Verify** that most modern beds are UVA-dominant (some emit UVB — phrase as "most," not "all").
- **Original angle:** Names the marketing incentive explicitly and separates UVA-tan from UVB-D, which the salon-side pages blur.
