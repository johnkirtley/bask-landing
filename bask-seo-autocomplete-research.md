# Bask landing-page SEO keyword research

Research date: July 19, 2026  
Market/context: Google.com in Safari, US session  
Pages reviewed: [getbask.app](https://www.getbask.app) and [Bask on the App Store](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/vitamin-d-sun-tracker-bask/id6758405235)

## Executive recommendation

Restructure the homepage around this search promise:

> **A vitamin D sun tracker that tells you the best time to go outside and how long to stay.**

The strongest homepage primary keyword is **vitamin D sun tracker**. It is a direct Google autocomplete phrase, precisely describes Bask, and is more differentiated than broad terms such as "UV index app" or "sunlight app."

The homepage should also answer the two high-intent questions Bask is uniquely built to solve:

- **how much sun for vitamin D**
- **best time to get vitamin D from sun**

Use **vitamin D tracker app**, **sun exposure tracker**, **sun exposure timer**, **UV tracker**, and **UV forecast** as supporting terms. Create a dedicated **vitamin D calculator** page because Google repeatedly expands that phrase into "sun," "location," and "UV index" variants that map directly to Bask's location-, weather-, skin-, clothing-, and UV-aware estimates.

## Current positioning versus search intent

### App Store listing

- Title: **Vitamin D Sun Tracker - Bask**
- Subtitle: **UV Index & Exposure Timer**
- Core promise: estimate vitamin D from sun exposure and supplements; identify the best time to go outside; time exposure using live UV, weather, skin, and clothing inputs.

The App Store metadata is already close to the recommended search language.

### Current website

- Title: **Bask: Vitamin D & Sun Tracker for iOS**
- H1: **Tired all the time, low mood, and no idea why?**

The title is reasonably aligned, but the H1 leads with a symptom hypothesis rather than the product category or the question the app answers. It also puts Bask closer to diagnostic territory. A search-aligned H1 can be clearer and safer without losing the emotional hook.

## Suggested homepage keyword architecture

### SEO title

**Vitamin D Sun Tracker & UV Exposure Timer | Bask**

### H1

**Know the best time to get Vitamin D from the sun.**

### Hero support copy

Bask is a vitamin D sun tracker for iPhone that uses your location, live UV forecast, weather, skin, and clothing to estimate how much sun you need and time safer outdoor sessions.

### Suggested section structure

1. **How much sun do you need for Vitamin D?**  
   Introduce the personalized estimate and explain why a universal minute count is misleading.

2. **Find the best time to get Vitamin D from the sun**  
   Show today's and tomorrow's UV-aware exposure windows.

3. **Track sun exposure with a live timer**  
   Explain live sessions, UV exposure, estimated IU progress, and burn-risk heads-ups.

4. **More than a UV index app**  
   Differentiate Bask from generic UV/weather apps using skin, clothing, clouds, sun angle, supplements, and the daily goal.

5. **A Vitamin D tracker for sun and supplements**  
   Explain the combined daily goal, magnesium/K2 helpers, Apple Health, streaks, and trends.

6. **FAQ built around Google questions**  
   Include "How much sun do I need for Vitamin D?", "What is the best time to get Vitamin D from the sun?", "Can you get Vitamin D through a window?", "Can you get Vitamin D from winter sun?", and "What UV index produces Vitamin D?"

The current tiredness/low-mood framing can remain below the hero as motivation, but it should not carry the H1 or imply Bask can diagnose the cause.

## Recommended page expansion

- `/vitamin-d-sun-tracker/` — product-intent page for the app, tracker, timer, and UV forecast
- `/vitamin-d-calculator/` — location-, UV-, skin-, clothing-, and weather-aware calculator landing page
- `/how-much-sun-for-vitamin-d/` — per-day, winter, production, and calculator questions
- `/best-time-vitamin-d-sun/` — best-time intent, with seasonal and location context
- `/vitamin-d-through-window/` — focused answer to a strong, repeated question
- `/vitamin-d-winter/` — winter sun, latitude, and supplement-planning education
- `/uv-index-vitamin-d/` — UV index, production, timing, and chart intent
- `/safe-sun-exposure/` — exposure time and Bask's safety guardrails
- `/sunburn-risk-calculator/` — only if the product can support the claim with appropriately cautious framing

The homepage should link to these pages contextually rather than trying to rank for every informational phrase itself.

## Terms to avoid leading with

- **sun tracker app** — heavily mixed with photography, gardening, solar-panel, and property intent
- **sunlight tracker** — similarly dominated by gardens, homes, maps, and solar use cases
- **sunlight app** — highly ambiguous and includes unrelated streaming and photography intent
- **vitamin D app** — too short and quickly drifts into supplements, appetite, and medical coding
- **UV index app** — relevant but generic; it hides Bask's vitamin D differentiation
- **tired all the time** — emotionally resonant, but broad, medically sensitive, and not a precise product query
- **vitamin D deficiency treatment** — Bask is educational and should not present itself as diagnosing or treating deficiency

## Medical and SEO guardrails

- Keep "estimate," "may," and "educational tool" language visible around vitamin D production and burn risk.
- Do not promise that a session fixes deficiency, fatigue, mood, or another symptom.
- Do not give a universal safe exposure duration; Bask's differentiator is that conditions and people vary.
- Cite qualified primary or public-health sources on informational pages, especially for deficiency, sunscreen, cancer risk, children, and supplement dosage.
- Keep the existing direction to seek testing or medical advice for suspected deficiency.

## Important limitation

Google autocomplete demonstrates live query phrasing and topic demand, but it does not provide search volume, ranking difficulty, or conversion rates. Validate the top clusters in Google Search Console and a keyword-volume tool before committing to a large content build. The strongest initial test is the homepage rewrite plus one focused **vitamin D calculator** page, because both align tightly with the product and create a clear internal-linking hub.
